Trenchless Sewer Repair in Queens NY

When it comes to sewer line issues, traditional repairs often involve digging up your yard or driveway, which can be both disruptive and costly. Fortunately, trenchless sewer repair in Queens NY offers a more efficient, less invasive solution. At Balkan Plumbing, we are proud to offer this cutting-edge technology that allows us to repair or replace your sewer lines with minimal disruption to your property.

Why Choose Trenchless Sewer Repair?

Minimal Disruption

Our trenchless methods require only small access points rather than extensive excavation, preserving your landscaping and hardscaping.

Faster Repairs

Traditional sewer repairs can take days or weeks. Trenchless repairs are typically completed in just a day or two.

Cost-Effective

While the upfront cost may be comparable, you'll save on landscaping restoration, driveway repairs, and other property damage costs.

Durability

Trenchless repairs often use modern materials that are more resistant to root intrusion, corrosion, and cracking than traditional pipes.

How Trenchless Sewer Repair Works

1

Pipe Bursting

This method involves breaking apart the old pipe while simultaneously pulling a new pipe into place, effectively replacing the damaged line without extensive digging.

2

Pipe Lining

Also known as Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP), this technique creates a new pipe within the old one using a resin-saturated liner that hardens in place.

Detailed Sewer Video Inspection

Our advanced camera technology allows us to see inside your pipes without digging.

The Clear View You Need

Video pipe inspection is a crucial diagnostic tool that helps us identify the exact location and nature of your sewer line problems. Using a specialized waterproof camera attached to a flexible rod, we can navigate through your pipes and transmit real-time video footage to a monitor.

This technology allows us to pinpoint issues such as:

  • Cracks and breaks in the pipe
  • Root intrusions
  • Blockages and obstructions
  • Pipe collapse
  • Joint separations
